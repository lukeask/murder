# Trademark and Branding Policy

This policy covers the murder project name, logos, wordmarks, crow artwork,
mascot artwork, and related branding assets.

The source code and project documentation are licensed under the MIT License, as
described in [LICENSE](LICENSE). The project name murder™, the project logo,
wordmark artwork, crow artwork, mascot artwork, and related brand assets are
owned by Luke Askew and are not licensed under MIT.

## Goals

The project is open source, and truthful references to it should be easy. The
purpose of this policy is to prevent confusion about what is official, endorsed,
sponsored, approved, or maintained by the murder project or Luke Askew.

## No Permission Required

You may use the murder name and related branding assets without asking
permission for truthful, non-misleading references to the project, including:

- Identifying, discussing, linking to, reviewing, criticizing, or comparing the
  official murder project.
- Screenshots, articles, videos, talks, package listings, tutorials, and other
  explanatory materials about murder.
- Compatibility statements, integrations, plugins, extensions, or tools that
  truthfully say they support, work with, were made with, or are compatible with
  murder.
- Forks, patches, mirrors, and source-available development work that clearly
  identify their relationship to the official murder project.
- Commentary, comparison, parody, fan art, and critical references that are not
  misleading. For example, you may depict your agentic development project's
  mascot standing victorious over murder's crow mascot, provided the context
  does not suggest the artwork is official, endorsed, sponsored, or approved by
  the murder project.

## Permission Required

You may not use the murder name, logos, wordmarks, crow artwork, mascot artwork,
or related branding assets in a way that is likely to confuse people about
source, sponsorship, affiliation, approval, endorsement, or official status.

In particular, you need permission to:

- Present a modified version, fork, product, package, service, or public build
  as the official murder project.
- Use the murder name or branding as the primary name, logo, icon, or visual
  identity of a separate project, product, package, service, company, or
  community.
- Use the murder name or branding to imply that Luke Askew or the murder project
  made, approved, sponsored, endorsed, or is responsible for a statement,
  roadmap, benchmark, release, integration, product, service, or organization
  where that is not true.
- Register domains, social handles, package names, app names, marks, or similar
  identifiers that are likely to be confused with official murder channels.

## Modified Versions

If you distribute a modified version as a separate project, product, package,
service, or public build, use a name and branding that clearly distinguish it
from the official murder project. You may truthfully state that your work is a
fork of, derived from, compatible with, or built using murder.

## Questions

For trademark or branding permission questions, contact Luke Askew through the
official repository or the package metadata contact address.
